Hidden Connections Synopsis
Full manuscript available on request

Lily is twenty. Pregnant and recently laid off, she gives birth alone. Three days later, her milk dries up from a sudden illness leaving her with no way to feed her baby. In 2037 America, there are no social safety nets, only for-profit systems. Low-cost formula companies have been replaced with genetically optimized options, four times the cost but free with insurance that Lily doesn't have. With prostitution recently legalized under the guise of ending sex trafficking and no family to call, Lily turns to a newly opened brothel for employment.

Among her clients is a man who calls himself Daniel. A mysterious man with little to say seeking emotional connection. He finds comfort in Lily and begins visiting regularly, twice a week. Trusting Daniel wouldn't ever hurt her, Lily finds herself strangely drawn to her new client developing an unspoken connection she can't explain. After he suddenly starts crying during a session, he leaves asking that she never speaks of it. She finds herself wondering if she'll ever see him again.

Lily's sister Alice has been in prison since she was seventeen, arrested after an armed robbery gone wrong orchestrated by her manipulative boyfriend Ryan, who blamed her at trial. Lily and Alice's mother is crippled by the news. Needing to provide for a family of three and now facing legal fees she feels pushed to the edge and cannot bear the shame of having a bright young daughter in prison. Unable to cope, she kills herself.

When Lily visits Alice in prison to break the news she discovers that Alice developed a transactional relationship with a guard named Steve. He promises her early release on good behavior in exchange for moving mysterious boxes from his car trunk into the formula line delivery trucks. Steve answers to another corrupt senior guard named Garrett, who initiates the entire process. He knows the boxes contain weapons, phones, SIM cards, and vials of a thick creamy substance that need to make their way to specific channels without a trace leaving the guards hand's clean.

The formula manufacturing business is owned by billionaire, Parker Tanner, who built his fortune across privatized prisons, hospitals, and longevity treatments. His obsession with immortality pushes him to focus on illegal human cloning and synthetic memory research so he can live forever. The women in the correctional facilities that manufacture his formula are his personal egg harvesting vessels that his Geneva research team requires to make progress.

Alice is released in 2036 with forty-seven dollars and nowhere to go. Too reluctant to reach out to Lily, she makes her way to the city's shelters to survive on her own. When she arrives, she finds they're at capacity and spirals quickly, turning to fentanyl. She is arrested on possession and loitering and trades information about prison smuggling to her arresting officer, giving up Garrett but protecting Steve. Meanwhile inside the correctional facility, Garrett is suddenly transferred to protect his role and Steve is promoted. The following week, Alice is picked up again on possession charges and sent to a holding cell. She's found unresponsive hours later. The coroner pronounces her dead. She overdosed in custody, a deliberate silencing that made its way through the system after she exposed the formula operation.

When Lily hears word about her sister dying in custody she discovers Daniel's true identity, Martin Davis, Warden of the correctional facility where Alice was held. Fearing she'll never see Daniel again and wanting answers, she decides to show up at the facility unannounced. Wanting to help, Steve gets Lily a room and calls the warden.

When confronted, Daniel pretends to not recognize Lily though she can tell she surprised him. Suspecting he's been unaware of the connection the entire time, Lily leaves without causing a scene.

The warden is called into Tanner's home office the following day. He has known about the brothel visits since they began, he owns the business through a private LLC. Parker shows the warden surveillance footage, and has him killed. A clone matching the warden is revealed to have synthetic memory and is sent to replace the deceased Martin Davis.

Lily returns home to Sophia, who is just beginning to roll over. Lily thinks about her sister, questioning why she never reached out for help. She wonders if Daniel is okay and hopes to see him again at the brothel. She holds her daughter and waits for her formula delivery.
